# Comprehensive Catalog of AML/CFT/Sanctions Red Flag, Typology and Indicator Documents — Global Reference (May 2026)

## TL;DR
- This catalog identifies 200+ authoritative red flag / typology / indicator publications from regulators, FIUs and law enforcement agencies across North America, Europe, Asia, Australia, and key international standard-setters. Coverage is concentrated 2018–2026, with landmark older sources retained where still authoritative (e.g., FATF 2006 TBML, FinCEN 2010 IVTS, FATF 2007 real estate).
- For US BSA/AML practitioners, the operationally critical core is: the FinCEN Advisory series (FIN-2014-A through FIN-2026-A001), OFAC's sanctions advisories (Russia/Iran maritime, ransomware, virtual currency), and FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual Appendix F. For non-US practitioners, prioritize FATF typologies plus the home-jurisdiction FIU's operational alerts.
- Highest-priority recent documents to harvest into transaction-monitoring and SMR/SAR systems: FinCEN Pig Butchering Alert (FIN-2023-Alert005, Sept 2023); FinCEN Iranian Oil Smuggling Advisory (FIN-2025-A002); FinCEN Chinese Money Laundering Networks Advisory (FIN-2025-A003); FATF "Complex Proliferation Financing and Sanctions Evasion Schemes" published 20 June 2025; AUSTRAC 2024 National ML/TF Risk Assessments; FINTRAC Operational Alert "Laundering the Proceeds of Illicit Synthetic Opioids," published 23 January 2025 by the trilateral North American Drug Dialogue Illicit Financing Working Group (FINTRAC/FinCEN/UIF Mexico) following analysis of 5,000 STRs; EBA/GL/2024/01 (16 January 2024, applicable from 30 December 2024) extending risk-factor guidelines to crypto-asset service providers; and OFAC's April 2025 updated Iranian Oil Sanctions Evasion Advisory.

## Key Findings

**Volume and concentration.** Authoritative red flag content is heavily concentrated in eight bodies — FinCEN, OFAC, FATF, AUSTRAC, FINTRAC, EBA, MAS/ACIP and HKMA — which collectively publish the majority of operationally usable indicators. Most other regulators reference or replicate this content with national context.

**Topical hot spots (2022–2026).**
- Russia sanctions evasion: FinCEN Alerts FIN-2022-Alert001/002/003; OFAC Russia Price Cap Advisory; OFAC Updated Foreign Financial Institution guidance under E.O. 14114; multilateral REPO Task Force Global Advisory.
- DPRK proliferation financing and IT workers: FATF June 2025 PF report (only 16% of FATF-assessed countries demonstrated high or substantial effectiveness on Immediate Outcome 11); FinCEN/OFAC/FBI joint DPRK IT workers advisory; OFAC DPRK shipping advisories.
- Pig butchering and relationship-investment scams: FinCEN FIN-2023-Alert005 (8 Sept 2023); AUSTRAC and FINTRAC equivalents.
- Iranian illicit oil and shadow banking: OFAC April 2025 maritime advisory; FinCEN FIN-2025-A002.
- Fentanyl precursors: FinCEN FIN-2024-A002; FINTRAC's 23 January 2025 Operational Alert.
- Crypto/VASPs: FATF Virtual Assets Red Flag Indicators (14 September 2020, based on 100+ case studies); EBA/GL/2024/01 (16 January 2024, in force 30 December 2024); AUSTRAC Digital Currencies financial crime guide (April 2022); FinCEN CVC Kiosks Notice 2025.

**Structural gaps.** PRC (PBOC), Vietnam (SBV), and many Eastern European FIUs publish little public English-language typology content. Asia-Pacific FIUs increasingly publish in local languages only.

**Format conventions.** US/Canada/Australia favor named indicator lists with SAR/STR/SMR key terms. EU bodies favor risk-factor guidelines (the EBA RFGL model). FATF favors longer typology reports with case studies. Wolfsberg/ACIP papers focus on industry best practices and questionnaires rather than discrete indicator lists.

## Recommendations (Decision-Ready, Staged)

**Stage 1 — Within 30 days (Foundation):**
1. Map your firm's risk profile to FATF's national priorities (Russia/Iran/DPRK sanctions, fentanyl, ransomware, pig butchering, TBML, BO concealment) and pull the corresponding 6–8 advisory documents into your transaction-monitoring rule library. Start with FinCEN FIN-2025-A002, FIN-2024-A002, FIN-2023-Alert005, OFAC's April 2025 Iranian Oil Advisory, and FATF's 20 June 2025 PF Schemes report.
2. For US-regulated entities, ensure SAR key terms from each in-scope FinCEN advisory are configured in the SAR engine, and produce a current crosswalk between active advisories and your typology library. Replace older 2014/2010 sources where superseded.
3. For multi-jurisdictional groups, designate a "primary" regulator's red flag list per booking center (HKMA in HK; MAS Notice 626 + ACIP in SG; CBUAE Rulebook in UAE; EBA RFGLs as amended in EEA) and a "secondary" reference set (FATF + Wolfsberg).

**Stage 2 — Within 90 days (Depth):**
4. Backfill DNFBP and sectoral coverage: real estate (FinCEN GTOs + Real Estate rulemaking; FATF 2022 RBA; AUSTRAC 2024 RA), VASPs (EBA/GL/2024/01; FATF VA Red Flags 2020; FINTRAC VC indicators; FIU-IND VASP User Manual), correspondent banking (Wolfsberg CBDDQ v1.4 + FCCQ v1.2; MAS guidance; CBUAE).
5. Build a fentanyl/synthetic opioids detection package combining FinCEN FIN-2024-A002 with FINTRAC's 23 January 2025 trilateral Operational Alert; specifically include indicators on imports of pill press equipment, precursor purchases from China-based chemical suppliers, and small-dollar wires consistent with the documented patterns.
6. For Russia/Iran sanctions, layer the OFAC + FinCEN + REPO Task Force + FINTRAC + FIU-Latvia indicator sets — they were explicitly designed to be used together.

**Stage 3 — Ongoing (Maintenance):**
7. Subscribe to RSS/email alerts from FinCEN, OFAC, FATF, AUSTRAC, FINTRAC, EBA, MAS, HKMA and FCA, and review every new advisory within 10 business days for coverage gaps.
8. Annually refresh the catalog mapping; flag superseded documents (e.g., the 2020 OFAC Iran maritime advisory was superseded by the April 2025 update; CBUAE issued a comprehensive AML/CFT/CPF guidance update in April 2026).

**Thresholds that change recommendations:**
- Direct Russia/Belarus/Iran/DPRK exposure or USD correspondent activity → prioritize OFAC sanctions advisories above all else.
- Real estate, casino, NPO or gatekeeper (lawyer/accountant) businesses → prioritize FATF sectoral guidance plus national equivalents.
- VASP operations → mandatory floor: FATF VA Red Flags 2020, EBA/GL/2024/01 (applicable from 30 December 2024), FINTRAC VC indicators, AUSTRAC digital currencies guide, FIU-IND VASP manual.

## Caveats

- **Translation risk:** Many Asian and continental European red flag documents (KoFIU, JAFIC, BaFin AuA, TRACFIN, UIF Italy, AMLO Thailand, PPATK) are authoritative only in their original language. English summaries (where available) often lag and may omit indicators.
- **Versioning:** This is a moving target. CBUAE issued a comprehensive update of its AML/CFT/CPF guidance in April 2026; BaFin's AuA 3.0 took effect 1 February 2025 (with a first amendment on 6 March 2025 reflecting the Financial Market Digitalisation Act and a second amendment on 2 July 2025 updating MLRO notifications); the OFAC Iran maritime advisory was reissued April 2025; and FATF released updated TF and PF reports in mid-2025. Always re-verify URLs to ensure you have the current version.
- **Non-public components:** FATF's 2018 Professional Money Laundering report has a non-public version available only to FATF/Egmont members with practical investigation recommendations; the public version is descriptive only. FinCEN distributes some Cyber Indicator Lists and 314(a) products only via the Secure Information Sharing System.
- **Format heterogeneity:** Some "documents" cited above are dynamic web pages with rolling indicator updates rather than static PDFs (e.g., AUSTRAC's sectoral indicator pages, FINTRAC's indicator pages, SEPBLAC's catalogue). Snapshot these on a regular cadence.
- **Coverage thinness:** Smaller European FIUs (Czech FAU, Hungary, Bulgaria, Romania), some Asian regulators (Vietnam SBV, China PBOC) and Latin American FIUs beyond Mexico are intentionally light because limited public English-language indicator content exists. Consult local-language portals directly.
- **Authority and enforceability:** Documents range from binding rules (FCA FCG, EBA Guidelines once incorporated by NCAs, BNM Policy Documents, MAS Notices) to non-binding guidance (FATF typologies, Wolfsberg standards, FINTRAC operational alerts). Internal policy mapping should distinguish.
- **No catalog substitutes for primary reading.** Indicator lists are the most-used pages of these documents but the surrounding case studies and methodology are what enable accurate alert disposition.
